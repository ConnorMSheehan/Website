About

Understanding the Social Architecture of Sleep

I am a sociologist and social demographer interested in understanding social inequality in people's health. Using large nationally representative datasets and advanced quantitative methods, I systematically document how social forces shape population-level health patterns.


My work pursues three overlapping questions: How can enhanced measurement improve our understanding of the sociological determinants of health? What are the sociological determinants of sleep in a world undergoing rapid transformation? And how does social inequality within families influence well-being and sleep?


Before joining ASU, I earned my Ph.D. in Sociology from The University of Texas at Austin (specializing in Demography) and completed a postdoctoral fellowship at USC's Leonard Davis School of Gerontology, supported by the National Institute on Aging.

Research

Three Questions Keeping Me Up

📐

Measurement & Methods

How can enhanced measurement improve our understanding of the sociological determinants of health? I investigate how the tools we use shape what we find, from self-reported sleep categorizations to latent variable approaches to health.

🌙

Social Determinants of Sleep

What are the sociological forces shaping sleep in a world undergoing rapid transformation? I study how education, neighborhoods, race/ethnicity, immigration, and political context influence sleep duration and quality at the population level.

👨‍👩‍👧

Families & Inequality

How does social inequality within families influence well-being and sleep? From spousal education to adult children's attainment, I examine how intergenerational transfers of advantage and disadvantage ripple through health.

Community Engagement

Data Devils

I co-founded Data Devils with Dr. Aaron Flores, a community analytics initiative that pairs ASU students with Arizona nonprofits to provide data-driven insights. The program trains students in applied research while delivering real, actionable analytical support to organizations working on the front lines of social issues across the state.


Our work has achieved statewide visibility and external funding, with projects spanning food insecurity mapping, pet adoption outcomes, women's crisis services, and basic needs assessment. We have partnered with all four of Arizona's major food bank networks, the Arizona Board of Regents, and municipal agencies including Maricopa County Animal Control. Our food insecurity dashboard training workshop, funded by ABOR and hosted at St. Mary's Food Bank, produced a 96% increase in participant confidence navigating the tool.


Data Devils has been featured by ASU News, Arizona PBS, KJZZ, the Arizona Board of Regents, Route Fifty, GovTech, and StateScoop.

  • Food Insecurity Geospatial dashboard mapping food insecurity at the census tract level across Arizona, built in partnership with St. Mary's Food Bank.
  • Pet Adoption Dashboard for Maricopa County Animal Control analyzing pet adoption outcomes and geographic patterns of dog licensing.
  • Women's Services Quantitative analysis of Hope Women's Center usage to inform resource allocation for women in crisis.
  • Basic Needs Tri-university basic needs data initiative with the Arizona Board of Regents and SHEEO.

Teaching

Courses

SOC 469 / GIS 469 / GIS 598

Multivariate Statistics for the Social Sciences

An interdisciplinary survey of quantitative methods, from regression to neural nets, taught entirely in R. Students build coding portfolios and model real data on everything from candy preferences to the Bechdel test.

SOC 448

Epidemics and Society

How social factors get "under the skin" during epidemics. Covers COVID-19, Ebola, the Black Death, and the opioid epidemic through the lens of social inequality.

SOC 390

Social Statistics I

Intro statistics with a hands-on twist. Students learn Excel, R, and how to be informed consumers of data. Final projects use real World Values Survey data.
